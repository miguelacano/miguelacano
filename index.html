About

For the past eight years I've been building frontend systems that live at the intersection of product thinking and technical depth. Most recently I spent four years at GoFundMe helping rebuild the Classy platform from the ground up into a complex micro-frontend architecture now powering enterprise nonprofit fundraising for thousands of organizations.

Before that, I was at Wolters Kluwer Enablon, modernizing a 20-year-old enterprise EHS platform and navigating the messy reality of post-acquisition UI unification across distributed teams in Chicago, Paris, and The Hague.

What makes me a little different: I came to engineering through a decade in digital strategy, content marketing, and communications. I know how to talk to product and design in their language, articulate trade-offs clearly, and build things that are actually meant for the people using them.

I'm currently building Cantura, a fullstack studio management app for private music teachers, and Appogia, a music practice intelligence platform exploring audio session capture and AI-assisted feedback loops. Both projects are sandboxes for production-grade architecture decisions in greenfield environments.

Projects

Cantura

Active · Fullstack · TypeScript

A studio management platform for private music teachers. Inspired by watching my wife manage her Suzuki violin studio with spreadsheets and notepads, Cantura is built around instrument-scoped access control, repertoire assignment and progress tracking, and a guardian-participatory notes system reflecting how Suzuki pedagogy actually works. Parents are active participants, not passive observers.

The architecture is production-shaped from the start: service layer authorization, strict invariants enforced in application logic, and a data model designed around user flows rather than database convenience.
